A novel method to greener carbon material for CO2 adsorption

Valorising agricultural waste into more valuable products is of crucial to develop a circular chemical economy, where less natural resources are used and wastes are effectively recycled. In this study, carbon derived from walnut shell demonstrates significant ability to capture CO2 from the atmosphere. More importantly, the result from this study can be translated into other agricultural waste materials.

Understanding the effects of cellulose fibers from various pre-treated barley straw on properties of aerogels

Agricultural byproducts such as barley straw, wheat straw and rice straw are common wastes in agricultural industry of many countries. Besides being burnt as fuel or being fed to cattle, these byproducts are renewable sources of cellulose fibres. This study looked further into how to separate cellulose from other elements in the straws. The separated cellulose was used as the precursor for fabricating aerogel - a spongy soft light material with great porosity. This aerogel material can be used to adsorb excess oil and dyes from water, therefore, enhance the quality of water sources.
